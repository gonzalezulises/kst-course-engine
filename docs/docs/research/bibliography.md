# Bibliography

The following references cover the foundational theory, computational methods, and applications of Knowledge Space Theory (KST). Entries are organized alphabetically by first author.

---

Birkhoff, G. (1937). Rings of sets. *Duke Mathematical Journal*, 3(3), 443--454.

Cosyn, E., & Uzun, H. (2009). Note on two necessary and sufficient axioms for a well-graded knowledge space. *Journal of Mathematical Psychology*, 53(1), 40--42.

Desmarais, M. C., & Baker, R. S. J. d. (2012). A review of recent advances in learner and skill modeling in intelligent learning environments. *User Modeling and User-Adapted Interaction*, 22(1--2), 9--38.

Doignon, J.-P. (1994). Knowledge spaces and skill assignments. In G. H. Fischer & D. Laming (Eds.), *Contributions to Mathematical Psychology, Psychometrics, and Methodology* (pp. 111--121). Springer.

Doignon, J.-P., & Falmagne, J.-C. (1985). Spaces for the assessment of knowledge. *International Journal of Man-Machine Studies*, 23(2), 175--196.

Doignon, J.-P., & Falmagne, J.-C. (1997). Well-graded families of relations. *Discrete Mathematics*, 173(1--3), 35--44.

Doignon, J.-P., & Falmagne, J.-C. (1999). *Knowledge Spaces*. Springer-Verlag.

Falmagne, J.-C., Cosyn, E., Doignon, J.-P., & Thiery, N. (2006). The assessment of knowledge, in theory and in practice. In B. Ganter & L. Nourine (Eds.), *Formal Concept Analysis* (LNAI 3874, pp. 61--79). Springer.
